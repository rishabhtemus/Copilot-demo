Messi Childhood

Lionel Messi was born on June 24, 1987 in Rosario, Argentina. He is the third of four children born to Jorge Messi, a steel factory manager, and his wife Celia Cuccittini, who worked in a magnet manufacturing workshop.

His family originates from the Italian city of Ancona and in 1883 his ancestors migrated to Argentina. Messi has a sister, Maria Sol and two older brothers, Rodrigo and Matias. He inherited his love for football from his father, who was a coach at a local youth football team. Messi started playing from an early age and his talent was soon apparent. However, at the age of 11, Messi was diagnosed with growth hormone deficiency (GHD). This was a condition that stunted growth and required expensive medical treatment, including the use of the drug Human growth hormone.

His parents were unable to pay for his treatment and they decided to move to Barcelona, Spain, where he joined the prestigious FC Barcelona youth academy. The club agreed to pay for his medical bills on the condition that he moved to Spain. Messi moved to Spain in 2000 and played for the FC Barcelona youth team, scoring almost 500 goals. He made his first team debut at the age of 17 in a friendly with Porto that marked the opening of the new Dragao stadium.
